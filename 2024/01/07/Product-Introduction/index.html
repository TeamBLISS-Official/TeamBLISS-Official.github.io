Product Introduction

3D model



We first used Solid Works 3D modeling software to design our prototype. The system comprises a detachable bin for empty bottles, a robotic arm, a water bottle positioning slot, a bottle cap twister, a photoelectric sensor, a camera, and a water reservoir.

The coordinate between the robotic arm and the bottle cap twister is designed to imitate the motion of human hands when opening a bottle.

Printed Circuit Board (PCB) Design

We used software Multisim to validate the logical feasibility of the circuit we designed and used PCB design software Altium Designer to create the Printed Circuit Board for our innovation.

Physical Prototype





We chose the STM32 chip as our core controller, and selected 30 kg servo motors to ensure sufficient torque for opening the bottle caps.

We chose the OpenMV image recognition module to take photos of bottles via the camera, and then we used YOLOv5, a cutting-edge deep learning recognition algorithm, to accurately identify various types of bottles.

We opted for photosensitive resin as the material for the frame and the water reservoir due to its waterproof characteristic.

We incorporated rubber material onto the bottle cap twister to increase friction between its contact surface and the bottle cap.
