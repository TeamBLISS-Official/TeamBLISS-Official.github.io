Technology

Photos of 3D models ?

  • Description: We first used Solid Works 3D (Hyperlink https://www.solidworks.com/) modeling software to design our prototype. The system comprises a detachable bin for empty bottles, a robotic arm, a water bottle positioning slot, a bottle cap twister, a photoelectric sensor, a camera, and a water reservoir.

  • The coordinate between the robotic arm and the bottle cap twister is designed to imitate the motion of human hands when opening a bottle.

Photos of Printed Circuit Board design in Altium Designer (https://www.altium.com/altium-designer ).

  • Description: We used software Multisim to validate the logical feasibility of the circuit we designed and used PCB design software Altium Designer to create the Printed Circuit Board for our innovation.

Photos of prototype ?

  • Description: We chose the STM32 chip as our core controller, and selected 30 kg servo motors to ensure sufficient torque for opening the bottle caps.

  • We chose the OpenMV (Hyperlink https://openmv.io/ ) image recognition module to take photos of bottles via the camera, and then we used YOLOv5, a cutting-edge deep learning recognition algorithm, to accurately identify various types of bottles.

  • We opted for photosensitive resin as the material for the frame and the water reservoir due to its waterproof characteristic.

  • We incorporated rubber material onto the bottle cap twister to increase friction between its contact surface and the bottle cap.

Benefit and Impact

  • Picture of recycled plastic bottles

  • Through automation, BLISS significantly enhances recycling efficiency by reducing the reliance on manual labor. This, in turn, leads to measurable cost savings for recycling stations.

  • The increased efficiency ensures a greater quantity of recycled materials, directly impacting the quantity of plastic bottles diverted from landfills.

  • A key advantage of BLISS is its contribution to preserving valuable non-renewable resources. By reducing unrecycled plastic, it mitigates environmental damage and prevents associated economic losses.

  • Our solution addresses the specific needs of high-traffic public spaces such as airports and malls, with the potential to impact a considerable number of users and plastic bottles on a daily basis.

  • One major reason people discard plastic bottles with liquids is the lack of immediate access to disposal facilities in public spaces such as water fountains and bathrooms. Since BLISS has a compact and cost-effective design, one can be placed right beside each trashcan in public spaces to provide immediate convenience for users, which translates into individual time savings.

  • The convenience offered by BLISS encourages a positive shift in user behavior, promoting a culture of responsible waste disposal and recycling. The presence of BLISS in public spaces also serves as a visible reminder of sustainable practices, raising awareness and educating users about the importance of environmental responsibility.

  • BLISS operates in a sterile environment, minimizing contact-associated risks and ensuring the well-being of users.

  • Emptied plastic bottles are cheaper and more sustainable to transport, contributing to overall environmental benefits.

  • The Environmental Protection Agency (HyperLink https://www.epa.gov/circulareconomy) set a goal to increase the recycling rate to 50% in 2030, and our innovation will be a major contribution to reaching that goal.
